Unit 1: EFFECTIVE CLASSROOM TRANSACTION

Paper 304 – Teaching Competence in English Language

INTRODUCTION

The role of teachers has changed significantly in modern education. Earlier, teachers were mainly providers of information. Today, they are expected to facilitate learning, encourage participation, and create opportunities for students to construct knowledge themselves. In language classrooms, especially English language classrooms, teachers must possess: Content knowledge, Communication skills, Transactional skills, Classroom management skills, Language proficiency. Effective classroom transaction is essential for successful language learning.

1. TEACHER TALK

Meaning

According to Longman's Dictionary (1992), "Teacher Talk is the special variety of language used by teachers while teaching learners."

What is Teacher Talk?

It refers to:

  • The language used by teachers in classrooms
  • Questions asked
  • Instructions given
  • Discussions initiated
  • Feedback and encouragement provided

Importance:

  • Character Formation
  • Knowledge Construction
  • Skill Development
  • Attitude Formation
  • Language Acquisition
  • Confidence Building

FUNCTIONS OF TEACHER TALK

  1. Generating Discussion: Creating opportunities for learners to think, speak, and interact.
  2. Asking Questions and Eliciting Responses: Elicitation means drawing responses from learners instead of directly giving answers.
  3. Reinforcing and Encouraging: Verbal (Good, Excellent) and Non-Verbal (Smile, Thumbs up).
  4. Giving Instructions and Directions: Should be clear, simple, easy to understand.
  5. Getting Feedback: Helps teachers identify learning gaps.
  6. Engaging in Formal Talk: Building rapport and creating a positive classroom atmosphere.

FEATURES OF GOOD TEACHER TALK

  1. Error-Free Language
  2. Comprehensible Language
  3. Audible Communication
  4. Learner-Friendly Nature
  5. Appropriate Speed
  6. Proper Pronunciation
  7. Appropriate Pause
  8. Proper Stress and Rhythm
  9. Proper Intonation
  10. Rich Vocabulary

DIALOGIC TEACHER TALK

Techniques: Agreeing or Disagreeing, Seeking Opinions, Using Different Sentence Types.

PARALINGUISTICS

Meaning

The term "Paralinguistic" means "beside language." It refers to aspects of communication other than words (Body language, Gestures, Facial expressions, Tone, Pitch).

Features:

  1. Volume
  2. Speed of Voice
  3. Intonation (Rising/Falling)
  4. Tone of Voice
  5. Pronunciation
  6. Articulation
  7. Pause
  8. Punctuation Awareness

STRATEGIES FOR BETTER TEACHER TALK

  1. Reduce Teacher Talk Time (TTT) and increase Student Talk Time (STT).
  2. Encourage Student Interaction
  3. Effective Questioning
  4. Effective Feedback
  5. Encourage Language Use
  6. Use Positive Language

NEED FOR ERROR-FREE TEACHER TALK

Teachers are language models. Students imitate words, sentences, pronunciation. Grammar, pronunciation, vocabulary should be correct.

UNIT 2: ASSESSMENT IN LANGUAGE LEARNING

INTRODUCTION

Assessment is a systematic process of collecting information about learners' progress, achievements, strengths, and weaknesses. The ultimate aim is to improve learning, support learners, and provide feedback.

TYPES OF ASSESSMENT

  1. Assessment as Learning: Learners themselves become active participants. They monitor learning, reflect on progress, and identify strengths/weaknesses. Promotes self-regulated learning.
  2. Assessment for Learning: Also known as Formative Assessment. Takes place during the learning process. Continuous, diagnostic, improvement-oriented.
  3. Assessment of Learning: Also known as Summative Assessment. Occurs after instruction is completed. Measures achievement and performance.

COMPARISON OF THREE TYPES OF ASSESSMENT

Aspect Assessment As Learning Assessment For Learning Assessment Of Learning
Focus Self-reflection Improvement Achievement
Conducted By Learner Teacher Teacher
Timing During learning During learning After learning
Purpose Self-monitoring Improve learning Measure achievement
Nature Reflective Formative Summative

PORTFOLIO ASSESSMENT

Meaning

A systematic collection of learner work that shows progress, achievements, and performance over time.

Contents: Written Assignments, Reading Records, Creative Writing, Projects, Teacher/Self/Peer Assessment Records.

Features of a Good Portfolio: Systematic, Comprehensive, Authentic, Continuous, Reflective.

SELF-ASSESSMENT & PEER ASSESSMENT

  • Self-Assessment: Learners evaluate their own performance. Benefits: Develops responsibility, confidence, reflection.
  • Peer Assessment: Learners assess the work of classmates. Benefits: Better understanding, collaboration, critical thinking.

CONTINUOUS AND COMPREHENSIVE ASSESSMENT (CCE)

System of continuous evaluation throughout the learning process. Continuous (regular observation, activities), Comprehensive (covers knowledge, skills, attitudes, values).

DEVELOPING ASSESSMENT TOOLS

Tools must have: Validity, Reliability, Objectivity, Practicality, Comprehensiveness.

Examples: Observation Schedule, Checklist, Rating Scale, Rubrics, Question Papers, Portfolio.

UNIT 3: ADAPTATION AND INCLUSION

INCLUSIVE EDUCATION

Meaning

A system where all students, including children with disabilities, learn together in general education classrooms. Principle: "Education for All".

Benefits: Increased confidence, better social skills, empathy, respect for diversity.

ROLE OF THE TEACHER IN INCLUSIVE EDUCATION

  1. Identifying Learner Needs
  2. Adapting Curriculum
  3. Providing Emotional Support
  4. Using Inclusive Strategies
  5. Using Assistive Technology
  6. Collaborating with Parents

CHILDREN WITH SPECIAL NEEDS (CWSN)

Examples: Visual/Hearing impairment, Intellectual disability, Learning disability (Dyslexia - reading, Dysgraphia - writing), Autism.

ADAPTATION

Meaning

Adjusting curriculum, teaching methods, learning materials to accommodate needs of all learners.

  • Modification: Changing learning outcomes, teaching methods, assignments to suit individual needs.
  • Accommodation: Adjusting instructional practices without changing learning goals (e.g., Audio materials, Extra time).

TECHNO-PEDAGOGY

Meaning

Integration of technology with pedagogy.

Assistive Technology: Screen Readers, Audio Books, Speech-to-Text Software.

MULTIPLE INTELLIGENCE (MI) THEORY

Proposed by Howard Gardner. Intelligence is not a single ability.

Types:

  1. Linguistic (words)
  2. Logical-Mathematical (reasoning)
  3. Visual-Spatial (pictures)
  4. Bodily-Kinesthetic (movement)
  5. Musical (rhythm)
  6. Interpersonal (people)
  7. Intrapersonal (self)
  8. Naturalistic (nature)

UNIT 4: EXPLORING SCHOOL RESOURCES

INTRODUCTION

Schools are not merely buildings where teaching takes place. They are rich resource centres that provide numerous opportunities for learning. School resources create an environment where learners can actively participate, collaborate, explore, and develop language skills. Effective utilization of school resources helps teachers make language learning meaningful, enjoyable, and learner-centred.

This unit focuses on:

  • School as a resource centre
  • Collaborative learning strategies
  • Action research
  • Developing reading texts
  • Features of reading materials

The unit emphasizes that language learning should extend beyond textbooks and utilize all available school resources.

LEARNING OUTCOMES

After studying this unit, a student teacher will be able to:

  1. Identify and Utilize School Resources: Recognize various resources available in schools and use them effectively for language learning.
  2. Implement Collaborative Learning: Plan and conduct collaborative learning activities.
  3. Solve Classroom Issues: Identify teaching-learning problems and develop solutions.
  4. Develop Reading Texts: Prepare reading materials according to learning outcomes.

MAJOR CONCEPTS

  • School as a Resource Centre: School assembly, School library, Class library, English clubs, Day celebrations, Reading corner, English fest.
  • Collaborative Learning Strategies: Group work, Games, Dramatization, Role play, Discussion, Debate, Projects, Seminars.
  • Action Research: Identifying classroom issues, Problem solving, Formal and informal strategies.
  • Development of Reading Texts: Features of good reading texts, Skills required for text development.

1. SCHOOL AS A RESOURCE CENTRE

Meaning of School Resources

School resources refer to all materials, facilities, people, and opportunities that support teaching and learning. They help simplify teaching and make learning more meaningful and understandable.

Types of School Resources:

  • Human Resources: Teachers, Students, Parents, Community members, Experts.
  • Material Resources: Books, Charts, Models, Libraries, Laboratories.
  • Physical Resources: School buildings, Classrooms, Playgrounds, Reading corners.
  • Technological Resources: Computers, Projectors, Smart boards, Internet facilities.
  • Community Resources: Local institutions, Resource persons, Cultural organizations.

Importance of School Resources:

  • Improve Learning: Learning becomes meaningful and practical.
  • Increase Participation: Children become active participants.
  • Enhance Language Skills: Resources provide opportunities for communication.
  • Promote Creativity: Learners express ideas creatively.
  • Support Inclusive Education: Resources help accommodate diverse learners.
  • Improve Motivation: Interesting resources increase learner interest.

IMPORTANT SCHOOL RESOURCES

2. SCHOOL ASSEMBLY

Meaning: School assembly is a gathering of students and teachers before the start of school activities.

Educational Value: Provides opportunities for Public Speaking, Listening Skills, Communication Skills, and Leadership Development.

Language Activities: News reading, Speech competitions, Thought for the day, Storytelling, English announcements, Quiz programmes.

3. SCHOOL LIBRARY

Meaning: A place where students, teachers, and parents have access to various reading materials and learning resources.

Objectives: Provide reading materials, support curriculum, develop reading habits, promote independent learning, enhance language development.

Features: Variety of books, Accessibility, Learner-centred, Curriculum support.

Functions: Serves as a reading centre, resource centre, information centre, and learning laboratory.

Benefits: Vocabulary development, Improved reading skills, Better writing, Critical thinking.

4. CLASS LIBRARY

Meaning: A small library established within individual classrooms.

Purpose: Promote reading habit, encourage independent reading, develop language skills, increase interest.

Features: Child-friendly books, Easy access, Student participation, Continuous use.

Role of Teacher: Select suitable books, encourage reading, organize reading sessions, monitor reading habits.

5. ENGLISH CLUBS

Meaning: School organizations that promote language learning through various activities.

Objectives: Develop language skills, build confidence, improve grammar sensitivity, enhance literary skills, develop leadership qualities.

Activities: Debate, Elocution, Storytelling, Quiz, Poetry Recitation, Role Play, Creative Writing, Group Discussions, Drama, Language Games.

Benefits: Improves communication skills, increases confidence, enhances creativity, promotes teamwork, encourages leadership.

6. ENGLISH DAY CELEBRATIONS

Meaning: A specific day celebrated where all interactions and activities take place in English.

Objectives: Promote English usage naturally, increase confidence, improve fluency, create a language environment.

Activities: Speech, Debate, Quiz, Drama, Skit, Storytelling, Poetry Recitation, Language Games.

7. READING CORNER

Meaning: A specially arranged space where learners can read books comfortably.

Purpose: Promote reading culture, encourage independent reading, improve language skills, create reading interest.

Features: Comfortable environment, attractive display of books, variety of reading materials, child-friendly atmosphere.

Benefits: Improves comprehension, enhances vocabulary, develops reading habit, supports language acquisition.

8. ENGLISH FEST

Meaning: A large-scale language event organized in schools.

Activities: Literary competitions, Drama, Debate, Quiz, Story Writing, Poetry Writing, Public Speaking, Language Exhibitions.

Benefits: Language Enrichment, Increased Participation, Confidence Building, Talent Identification.

9. BIODIVERSITY PARK

Meaning: An area within the school that preserves various plants and natural resources.

Language Learning Possibilities: Students can observe nature, write descriptions, prepare reports, develop vocabulary.

Benefits: Experiential Learning, Environmental Awareness, Creative Writing Opportunities, Observation Skills.

COLLABORATIVE LEARNING

Meaning

An educational approach in which learners work together to solve problems, complete tasks, or create products.

Characteristics: Group-Based Learning, Shared Responsibility, Active Participation, Peer Interaction, Cooperative Problem Solving.

Advantages: Improves Communication, Develops Social Skills, Encourages Critical Thinking, Builds Team Spirit, Enhances Learning.

COLLABORATIVE LEARNING STRATEGIES

  1. Group Work: Students work in small groups. Advantages: Cooperation, Peer learning. Limitations: Unequal participation.
  2. Games: Educational games support language learning. Advantages: Fun, Motivation. Limitations: May distract.
  3. Dramatization: Students act out situations. Advantages: Improves speaking, builds confidence. Limitations: Time consuming.
  4. Role Play: Students assume different roles. Advantages: Communication skills, creativity. Limitations: Hesitation.
  5. Discussion: Exchange of ideas. Advantages: Critical thinking. Limitations: Dominance by a few.
  6. Debate: Structured argument. Advantages: Logical thinking, fluency. Limitations: Requires preparation.
  7. Project Method: Investigate real-life issues. Advantages: Experiential learning, research skills. Limitations: Time and resources.
  8. Seminar: Students present topics. Advantages: Public speaking, subject mastery. Limitations: Anxiety among learners.

ACTION RESEARCH

Meaning

Action Research is a systematic process used by teachers to identify classroom problems and develop practical solutions.

Need for Action Research: To solve problems like poor reading skills, low participation, lack of motivation, learning difficulties.

Objectives: Identify Problems, Analyze Causes, Develop Solutions, Improve Classroom Practices, Enhance Learning Outcomes.

Steps in Action Research:

  1. Identify Problem
  2. Collect Data
  3. Analyze Data
  4. Plan Action
  5. Implement Action
  6. Evaluate Results
  7. Reflect

Benefits: Professional Development, Better Classroom Management, Improved Learning, Resource Mobilization.

DEVELOPING READING TEXTS

Meaning

Teachers should be able to create reading materials based on expected learning outcomes.

Characteristics of a Good Reading Text:

  • Appropriate Language Level: Suitable for learner age.
  • Meaningful Content: Connected to learner experience.
  • Interesting Theme: Maintains learner interest.
  • Clear Structure: Easy to follow.
  • Suitable Vocabulary: Neither too difficult nor too easy.
  • Learning Outcome-Oriented: Supports intended objectives.

Skills Required for Developing Reading Texts:

Understanding Learner Needs, Selecting Appropriate Content, Organizing Ideas Logically, Using Suitable Vocabulary, Maintaining Coherence, Integrating Learning Outcomes.

Significance: Reading texts help improve comprehension, develop vocabulary, enhance language skills, promote independent learning, and build critical thinking.
